Music Production (Introductory Level)

This entry-level course introduces the essential concepts of music production, including audio post-production, mixing, recording, synthesizers, and file management. Students will gain hands-on experience working with Pro Tools, learning how to use its stock plugins to build a solid foundation in sound creation, editing, and mixing techniques. By the end of the course, students will have a basic understanding of the entire music production process from start to finish.

Introduction to Music Production

  • Gain a foundational understanding of music production, from recording to mixing and post-production techniques
  • Hands-on experience with Pro Tools, where students will learn how to use its stock plugins for various aspects of sound creation, editing, and mixing
  • An introduction to the practical use of synthesizers in music production and how to manage audio files effectively during the production process
  • Learn the key skills needed for sound design, editing, and mixing, which will serve as a basis for more advanced music production courses

Required Hardware

  • MacBook Pro : A reliable, powerful computer is essential for running Pro Tools and handling audio editing software efficiently.
  • Mixing Headphones : High-quality headphones are necessary for accurate audio monitoring during the recording and mixing process. Closed-back options:

    Beyerdynamic DT 770, Audio-Technica ATH-M50X, or similar models, perfect for providing isolation during mixing and recording.

    Open-back options: Sennheiser HD 650, Beyerdynamic DT-770 PRO, or similar models, ideal for an open, natural sound ideal for critical listening.

  • iLok : A USB dongle required for managing and storing licenses for Pro Tools and various plugins used in the production process.
Certification:
  • Upon successful completion of the course and assessments, students will receive a certificate of completion. This certificate is a valuable addition to a portfolio and can help demonstrate acquired skills in music production, which may be useful for pursuing further education or career opportunities in the field.
